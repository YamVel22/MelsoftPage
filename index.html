About Us

We are a team of experienced software developers and educators who are passionate about helping people learn the skills they need to succeed in the tech industry.

  • Online or Onsite Learning
  • Community Driven
  • Team Programming

Melsoft academy stems from the award winnning software engineering company. Driven by the same pioneering drive and knowledge that distinguished Melsoft in the technology sector, our academy is dedicated to combating youth unemployment in Africa by providing students with vital tech skills, practical work exposure, and employment opportunities. Crafted as immersive bootcamp experiences, our specialized programs aim to enable the upcoming cohort of software developers, emphasizing hands-on learning and community involvement. With our commitment to income-share agreements and job assurances, we strive to eliminate financial obstacles and pave the way for rewarding and prosperous tech professions for young individuals throughout the continent.

Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to the FAQ page for our coding boot camp! We hope that this page will help answer any questions you may have about our program.

  • Who is the bootcamp for?

    Our bootcamp is suitable for anyone who is interested in learning to code, regardless of their prior experience. We have had students from a variety of backgrounds, including those who have never written a line of code before, as well as those who have some programming experience and want to take their skills to the next level.

  • What are the entry requirements?

    You must be over 16 and know how to use a mouse and keyboard. The determination and willingness to learn cannot be lacking either.

  • What are the available payment options?

    We offer several payment methods for our students. Please check out the pricing page for more information.

  • Where are the lessons held?

    Our coding boot camp is held through our online classroom experience. Students also meet routinely at physical networking events and career support seminars.

  • What is the schedule like?

    The bootcamp is both a full-time and part-time program. Full time students should expect to spend 40 hours per week in class and on coursework whereas part-time students can complete the program at their own pace (recommend 20 hours a week). Class hours are typically from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm, with breaks throughout the day.
